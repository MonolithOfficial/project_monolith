# The Making of Worldline:Dead

When we first started working on what would become *Worldline:Dead* in 2013, the only thing at our hands was the title track, then-called *Epilogue One*. For 5 years, this track sat untouched, until early 2018, when it became a starting point of something more. What started as a singular track meant to end something that hadn't even started evolved into a fully realized conceptual album over the course of six months. 

## The Concept

The album title came from a physics term — a worldline is the path of an object through spacetime. The idea of a worldline dying felt like the right metaphor for the themes we were exploring: loss, transformation, and the weight of choices that can never be undone. The term came to us through animated series called *Steins;Gate*. In this series, there are multiple worldlines, each with different realities, none of which are *Dead* - that is *Worldline;Dead*.

## Recording Process

We tracked the entire album at Monolith Terminal 1 studio, although using little to none of its gear. Worldline;Dead was not recorded, but rather assembled - it's a frankenstein of random samples, stitched together with no transitions whatsoever.

There is a certain unpredictability to this album. Some sections of it drag along, yet others are too fleeting. Some tracks like *Watermelon's Symphony* go through several phases, almost seeming like three songs strung together.

## Key Tracks

**Memories** — This was one of the last tracks assembled for Worldline;Dead sessions along with *As of Right Now*. These tracks clearly differ in tone from the rest of the album, being very calm... almost serene. Upon experiencing the whole album, one might see these tracks as "calm before the storm" and come to miss it by the time the static of the title track takes hold.

**The Impact** — While other tracks in *Worldline;Dead* might seem rather scattered, this one emerges as the most thematically cohesive, having its drama and purposeful suspense last till the very last second.

**Worldline:Dead (title track)** — While predating all the other tracks on this album by at least 5 years, the title track is meant to be a full stop, a halt to all music by all-consuming static. A definitive end of the worldline. 

## Mixing and Mastering

The distant sound the low volume of *Worldline;Dead* largely comes from the low quality of the samples. It's a one-off, detached, isolated sound that stays unique in Project Monolith's discography to this day. 

We like to imagine that there's a dance between the the deep, dark bass and the bright, yet poignant, hopeful leads in this album that represents the combinations of high and low moments of one's life before its end.

## Conclusion

We believe *Worldline;Dead* is a product of spontaneity, of listening to your ideas, letting them flow unrestricted. In essence, with all its flaws, this album is simply human.
